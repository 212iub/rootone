Design Elements Website Examples



Listed below are websites that exemplify C.R.A.P. design strategies.

C.R.A.P; Contrast, Repetion, Alignment, Proximity


Contrast - Atavist Magazine
  • Contrast describes the visual difference between objects close in association.
    Using ocular cues guided by contrast, a designer can direct the viewer's attention
    to specific areas of a webpage to express importance. Contrast is produced
    through the juxtaposition of design elements like color, font, size, and texture/pattern.



  • On Atavist Magazine’s site, audiences read entertaining long form stories submitted by
    authors globally. Their webpage finds its own style, presenting original stories with
    emphasis on illustrations scattered throughout each story. As seen in the images of the
    article, when reading a story on AM, attention towards the images is drawn by the relatively
    muted surrounding colors and fonts.

Repetition - Moma
  • Repetition of design elements is utilized to build a sense of unity within an art
    form, in this case a website. Continual use of logos, catch phrases, typography, overall style,
    etc… provides viewers with consistency and eventually, familiarity.



  • MoMA’s opening page seems to aim at informing potential visitors of their current art
    collection. This is executed by the repetition of an artist's name beside their work.

Alignment - Artist Michelle Carlos' Website Portfolio
  • Proper alignment in design creates space that respects borders and margins. Its purpose is to
    forge organization while visually indicating groups of elements without actual text. The element
    is noticeable across most informational content, and is likely something people perpetuate
    unconsciously.



  • Artist Michelle Carlos’ website showcases her vast illustration and design art portfolio,
    organizing each of her works using alignment. By aligning her art pieces closely and beside
    each other, she creates an eye-catching digital gallery easy for new website visitors to
    navigate.
